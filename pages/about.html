About Veronica Miramontes

Chicana Indigenous Educator and Advocate for Monterey Peninsula Schools

My Story

Heritage

45% of my ancestors are from the Indigenous Americas, predominantly NorthEastern Mexico (Huachichil Nation) and SouthEast Texas (Coahuiltecan Nation). I proudly identify as Chicana Indigenous, embracing both my Latina and Indigenous roots.

Education

I hold a Bachelor's degree in Education from California State University, Monterey Bay, and a Master's in Educational Leadership with a focus on culturally responsive teaching and community engagement.

Career

My career began as an educator in local schools, where I developed a deep understanding of our community's needs. This experience fuels my passion for serving on the Monterey Peninsula Schools Board.

Achievements

  • Implemented successful bilingual education programs
  • Led initiatives to increase parent involvement in schools
  • Developed partnerships with local businesses for student internships

"I am tatli (compassion), anantli (women of the river), teyacanqui (leader) and happy. I am Chicana, I am Indigenous. I am Chicana Indigenous."
